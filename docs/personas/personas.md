## Persona 1: David Chen — Australian Property Investor

### Background
David is a 48-year-old financial planner based in Melbourne. He owns two investment properties in Victoria and is looking to diversify internationally. He visited Japan for skiing three years ago and has been casually researching Japanese property since. He has no Japanese language skills and relies entirely on English-language resources.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 48
- **Location:** Melbourne, Australia
- **Occupation:** Financial Planner (self-employed)
- **Income:** A$180K+/year
- **Tech comfort:** Moderate (uses property apps, reads financial blogs)
- **Language:** English only

### Goals
- Understand which areas in Japan offer the best investment returns for foreign buyers
- Compare areas based on yield potential, lifestyle appeal, and entry price
- Find a trustworthy source of information that is not trying to sell him something immediately
- Make an informed decision before engaging a property company

### Frustrations
- Most Japan property sites are listing portals with no educational content
- Hard to compare areas without local knowledge
- Concerned about hidden costs, legal complexity, and language barriers
- Skeptical of sites that push hard for contact details before providing value

### Behaviors
- Researches extensively before committing (3 to 6 months research cycle)
- Saves articles and properties to compare later
- Uses budget filters to narrow options
- Reads case studies and success stories for validation
- Visits the site 2 to 3 times per week during active research

### Key Features Used
1. **Area pages** — Reads detailed guides for Hakuba, Niseko, Karuizawa
2. **Comparison tool** — Compares 2 to 3 areas side by side on price, lifestyle, access
3. **Budget selector** — Filters content to ¥15M to ¥30M range (A$150K to A$300K)
4. **Save/bookmark** — Saves area pages and articles for later reference
5. **Area preference quiz** — Takes quiz to discover areas he has not considered
6. **Contact form** — Submits inquiry after 4 to 6 weeks of research

### User Journey
1. **Discover** — Finds Japanoma via Google search "buying property in Hakuba for Australians"
2. **Explore** — Reads Hakuba area page, saves it, browses related articles
3. **Compare** — Uses comparison tool to evaluate Hakuba vs Karuizawa vs Niseko
4. **Deepen** — Takes area preference quiz, discovers Matsumoto as an option
5. **Budget** — Selects ¥15M to ¥30M range, filters content accordingly
6. **Engage** — Submits contact form with specific area interests

### Success Metrics
- Returns to the site 3+ times before submitting a contact form
- Saves 5+ items across multiple sessions
- Completes at least one quiz
- Contact form submission includes specific area preferences

## Persona 2: Yuki Tanaka — Japanese-Australian Buyer

### Background
Yuki is a 35-year-old marketing manager born in Sydney to Japanese parents. She speaks conversational Japanese and visits family in Osaka annually. She is considering buying a small property near her grandmother's hometown in Nara Prefecture as both a family connection and a future rental income source. She understands both Australian and Japanese property markets at a surface level.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 35
- **Location:** Sydney, Australia
- **Occupation:** Marketing Manager at a tech company
- **Income:** A$120K/year
- **Tech comfort:** High (digital native, uses multiple apps)
- **Language:** English (primary), Japanese (conversational)

### Goals
- Find a property near Nara/Osaka that connects her to her heritage
- Balance emotional attachment to specific areas with practical investment logic
- Understand renovation requirements for older Japanese homes
- Find content in English with Japanese area names she can verify with family

### Frustrations
- Existing English resources oversimplify Japan ("just buy an akiya for $10K!")
- Wants nuanced area information, not tourist-level guides
- Finds it hard to get renovation cost estimates for specific design styles
- Does not want to be treated as a "foreign buyer" — she has cultural context

### Behaviors
- Browses with specific areas in mind (Nara, Osaka periphery)
- Interested in design style content (wabi-sabi, traditional Japanese)
- Shares articles with family members in Japan for their input
- Compares property types (akiya vs apartment) more than areas
- Visits the site 1 to 2 times per week, often on mobile during commute

### Key Features Used
1. **Area pages** — Deep reads on Nara Prefecture, Osaka periphery areas
2. **Design style quiz** — Confirms her preference for wabi-sabi/traditional Japanese
3. **Content hub** — Reads articles on renovation, akiya process, legal guides
4. **Save/bookmark** — Saves articles to share with family
5. **Use case quiz** — Explores "Dual-Location Living" use case content
6. **Budget selector** — Filters to ¥5M to ¥15M range

### User Journey
1. **Discover** — Finds via Google search "buying akiya in Nara Prefecture"
2. **Deep dive** — Reads Nara area page, appreciates Japanese name annotations (nameJa)
3. **Style** — Takes design style quiz, gets "Traditional Japanese" result
4. **Learn** — Reads renovation articles, saves guides about akiya purchase process
5. **Share** — Bookmarks content to discuss with family on next Osaka visit
6. **Contact** — Submits inquiry mentioning specific Nara areas and family connection

### Success Metrics
- Engages deeply with content (high time-on-page, multiple article reads)
- Quiz completion with detailed responses
- Saves content and returns across multiple sessions
- Contact form includes nuanced, specific information

## Persona 3: Kaz & Shiun — Go&C Admin Users

### Background
Kaz Yasumura is the Director of Go&C Partners, a Japan-based property company targeting Australian investors. Shiun is the Chief Marketing Officer responsible for content strategy and marketing. Together, they need to understand what Australian buyers want so they can make informed decisions about property acquisition, renovation direction, and marketing spend.

### Demographics
- **Kaz:** Director, Japan-based, bilingual (Japanese/English), business-focused
- **Shiun:** CMO, marketing background, will manage content post-launch, strategic thinker
- **Tech comfort:** Moderate (comfortable with business tools, not developer tools)
- **Primary device:** Laptop for dashboard, occasionally mobile for quick checks

### Goals
- Identify which areas have the highest buyer demand before investing in property acquisition
- Understand buyer use case preferences to guide renovation decisions
- Track design style trends to inform interior design choices
- Validate pricing strategy against buyer budget preferences
- Publish and manage content independently without developer assistance
- Export reports for internal strategy meetings and investor presentations

### Frustrations
- Currently rely on anecdotal feedback from buyers, not data
- Cannot see demand signals until a buyer contacts them (too late for planning)
- Need to justify acquisition decisions to investors with data
- Content updates currently require a developer (unacceptable post-launch)
- Want actionable insights, not raw data dumps

### Behaviors
- Check admin dashboard 2 to 3 times per week
- Review area demand trends before property viewing trips
- Export monthly reports for strategy meetings
- Compare area performance over time to validate decisions
- Publish 1 to 2 content pieces per week via CMS

### Key Features Used (Admin Dashboard)
1. **Overview metrics** — Quick snapshot of total signals, active users, top areas
2. **Area demand chart** — Line chart of area interest over time, filterable by date range
3. **Use case distribution** — Pie chart showing buyer intention breakdown
4. **Design preferences** — Bar chart of style popularity from quiz data
5. **Price range analysis** — Histogram of budget distribution
6. **Cross-tabulation heatmap** — Area × Property Type, Area × Use Case matrices
7. **CSV/PDF export** — Download data for presentations and strategy meetings

### Key Features Used (CMS)
1. **Article publishing** — Write and publish area guides, investment articles
2. **Taxonomy tagging** — Tag content with area, property type, use case, design style, price range
3. **Media management** — Upload property photos with automatic optimization
4. **Content scheduling** — Draft now, publish later

### User Journey (Dashboard)
1. **Morning check** — Open dashboard, scan overview metrics for the past week
2. **Deep dive** — Click into area demand, filter to last 30 days, compare Hakuba vs Niseko
3. **Cross-reference** — Open cross-tabulation view, Area × Use Case, to see what Hakuba visitors are interested in
4. **Export** — Download CSV of area demand data for upcoming investor meeting
5. **Action** — Use insights to prioritize next property viewing trip to Hakuba

### User Journey (CMS)
1. **Draft** — Shiun writes a new article about Karuizawa lifestyle in Sanity Studio
2. **Tag** — Selects Karuizawa area, Seasonal Living use case, Japandi design style
3. **Media** — Uploads 5 property photos; Sanity auto-optimizes for web
4. **Preview** — Reviews the article via preview link before publishing
5. **Publish** — Clicks Publish; article appears on live site within seconds via ISR

### Success Metrics
- Dashboard loads in < 3 seconds
- Can identify top 3 areas by demand in under 2 minutes
- Content published weekly without developer assistance
- Export reports in under 30 seconds
- Cross-tabulation insights inform at least one business decision per month

## Persona 4: Emma Westbrook — Casual Browser/Researcher

### Background
Emma is a 29-year-old graphic designer in Brisbane who has always been fascinated by Japanese culture. She recently watched a documentary about renovating abandoned houses in Japan and started Googling. She has no concrete plans to buy property but is curious and enjoys exploring the possibility. She represents the majority of Japanoma visitors: early-stage researchers who may or may not convert.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 29
- **Location:** Brisbane, Australia
- **Occupation:** Freelance Graphic Designer
- **Income:** A$75K/year
- **Tech comfort:** High (design tools, social media, visual platforms)
- **Language:** English only

### Goals
- Satisfy curiosity about buying property in Japan
- Understand what is realistically possible on a modest budget
- Explore Japanese design aesthetics (professional interest in Japandi)
- Take quizzes for fun and share results
- Bookmark interesting content for "someday" reference

### Frustrations
- Most property sites assume you are ready to buy (she is not)
- Wants visual, engaging content, not dense legal text
- Does not want to give her email just to browse
- Feels overwhelmed by the complexity of buying property abroad

### Behaviors
- Browses casually, often on mobile, often in the evening
- Takes quizzes as entertainment; shares results on social media
- Saves content to a "Japan dream" bookmark folder
- Visits the site irregularly (once every 1 to 2 weeks)
- Reads design and lifestyle content more than investment content
- May never register or submit a contact form

### Key Features Used
1. **Design style quiz** — Takes it for fun, shares "Dark Japandi" result
2. **Area pages** — Browses visual area pages for inspiration
3. **Content hub** — Reads lifestyle articles, design inspiration pieces
4. **Budget selector** — Explores "Under ¥5M" range out of curiosity
5. **Save/bookmark** — Saves 2 to 3 items to localStorage (never registers)

### User Journey
1. **Discover** — Finds via social media link or Google search "cheap houses in Japan"
2. **Engage** — Takes design style quiz, enjoys the visual experience
3. **Browse** — Explores area pages with beautiful photography
4. **Dream** — Reads an article about "Wabi-sabi Renovation on a Budget"
5. **Save** — Bookmarks a few items, closes the tab
6. **Return** — Comes back 2 weeks later, browses more content
7. **No conversion** — May never register or contact, but generates valuable anonymous signals

### Why Emma Matters
Emma represents the largest user segment. Her anonymous interactions (quiz responses, area views, design preferences, budget exploration) generate the taxonomy-tagged events that power the admin dashboard. Even though she may never become a buyer, her behavior patterns reveal market trends:

- Her quiz result ("Dark Japandi") contributes to design preference data
- Her area browsing (Hakuba, Niseko) contributes to area demand metrics
- Her budget exploration (Under ¥5M) informs pricing strategy
- Her content saves indicate which articles resonate

**Every anonymous signal has business value.** The platform is designed to extract insights from Emma's casual browsing just as effectively as from David's intentional research.

### Success Metrics
- Takes at least one quiz per visit
- Views 3+ pages per session
- Returns at least once within 30 days
- Generates taxonomy-tagged events that contribute to aggregated analytics
